# Weekly Gym Workout Plan
### UCF Recreation & Wellness Center — Beginner Strength & Stamina Program

> **Student profile:** First-year Engineering undergraduate, beginner, building strength and stamina from scratch.
> **Morning gym slot:** 06:00–07:00 daily at RWC Main Campus.
> **Evening cardio slot:** 17:30–18:30 daily (jogging / running / cycling / swimming) — separate from gym.
> **Intensity note:** Morning sessions are kept at moderate load to preserve energy for the evening session.

---

## Weekly Overview

| Day | Focus | Intensity |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Core & Functional Strength | Moderate |
| Tuesday | Upper Body — Push (Chest · Shoulders · Triceps) | Moderate |
| Wednesday | Lower Body (Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves) | Moderate |
| Thursday | Back Strength (Lats · Traps · Rear Delts) | Moderate |
| Friday | Arms & Biceps | Light–Moderate |
| Saturday | Full Body Light Circuit | Light |
| **Sunday** | **REST DAY** | — |

**Session structure every day:** 8–10 min warm-up · 40–45 min main workout · 8–10 min cool-down
**Beginner parameters:** 3 sets × 12–15 reps · 60–90 sec rest between sets · focus on form over weight

---

## Monday — Core & Functional Strength

> Builds the foundation. A strong core protects the spine, improves posture, and transfers power to every other lift.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- 5 min light treadmill walk (pace 4–5 km/h)
- Dynamic stretches: leg swings × 10 each side · hip circles × 10 · arm circles × 10

**Main Workout (42 min)**

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps / Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Plank | 3 | 45 sec hold | Elbows under shoulders, keep hips level |
| 2 | Dead Bug | 3 | 12 each side | Slow and controlled, lower back stays flat |
| 3 | Cable Crunch or Crunch Machine | 3 | 15 | Squeeze abs at bottom, don't pull with neck |
| 4 | Pallof Press (cable anti-rotation) | 3 | 12 each side | Stand tall, resist rotation |
| 5 | Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (light) | 3 | 12 | Hinge at hips, soft knees, feel hamstrings |
| 6 | Bird Dog | 3 | 12 each side | Opposite arm + leg, pause at top |
| 7 | Farmer's Carry (dumbbells) | 3 | 30 m walk | Stand tall, shoulders back |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Child's pose — 30 sec
- Cat-cow — 10 reps
- Lying spinal twist — 30 sec each side
- Hip flexor kneeling stretch — 30 sec each side

---

## Tuesday — Upper Body Push (Chest · Shoulders · Triceps)

> Builds pushing strength — chest, front shoulders, and triceps working together.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- Arm circles forward and backward × 15 each
- Band pull-aparts × 15 (or doorway stretch)
- 5 min light rowing machine (easy pace)

**Main Workout (42 min)**

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dumbbell Bench Press | 3 | 12 | Control the descent, don't bounce off chest |
| 2 | Incline Dumbbell Press | 3 | 12 | 30–45° incline, targets upper chest |
| 3 | Cable Chest Fly | 3 | 15 | Wide arc, squeeze chest at midline |
| 4 | Dumbbell Shoulder Press | 3 | 12 | Seated or standing, don't arch lower back |
| 5 | Dumbbell Lateral Raise | 3 | 15 | Light weight, slight bend in elbow |
| 6 | Tricep Rope Pushdown (cable) | 3 | 15 | Elbows pinned to sides, full extension |
| 7 | Overhead Dumbbell Tricep Extension | 3 | 12 | Both hands on one dumbbell, keep elbows in |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Chest doorway stretch — 30 sec each arm position
- Shoulder cross-body stretch — 30 sec each side
- Tricep overhead stretch — 30 sec each side
- Neck rolls — slow, 5 each direction

---

## Wednesday — Lower Body (Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves)

> Legs are the biggest muscle group. Training them boosts metabolism and builds overall athleticism.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- 5 min stationary bike (easy pace, no resistance)
- Leg swings front-back × 10 each leg
- Bodyweight squats × 10 (slow)

**Main Workout (42 min)**

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Goblet Squat (dumbbell) | 3 | 15 | Chest tall, knees track over toes |
| 2 | Leg Press Machine | 3 | 12 | Feet shoulder-width, don't lock knees at top |
| 3 | Dumbbell Walking Lunge | 3 | 10 each leg | Long stride, back knee nearly touches floor |
| 4 | Seated Leg Curl Machine | 3 | 12 | Full range of motion, no jerking |
| 5 | Glute Bridge (bodyweight) | 3 | 15 | Drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top |
| 6 | Standing Calf Raise | 3 | 20 | Slow — 2 sec up, 2 sec hold, 2 sec down |
| 7 | Step-Ups (box + dumbbells) | 3 | 10 each leg | Full hip extension at top |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Standing quad stretch — 30 sec each leg
- Hamstring stretch (seated or standing) — 30 sec each leg
- Pigeon pose — 45 sec each side
- Calf stretch on wall — 30 sec each leg

---

## Thursday — Back Strength (Lats · Traps · Rear Delts)

> A strong back protects posture — especially important for long hours at a desk or library.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- 5 min rowing machine (light pace, focus on form)
- Cat-cow × 10 reps
- Shoulder external rotations × 15 each arm

**Main Workout (42 min)**

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lat Pulldown (cable) | 3 | 12 | Pull to upper chest, lean back slightly |
| 2 | Seated Cable Row | 3 | 12 | Elbows drive back, squeeze shoulder blades |
| 3 | Dumbbell Single-Arm Row | 3 | 12 each | Flat back, elbow drives to hip |
| 4 | Face Pull (cable rope) | 3 | 15 | External rotation at the end, elbows high |
| 5 | Dumbbell Bent-Over Rear Delt Fly | 3 | 15 | Slight bend in knees, arms arc out wide |
| 6 | Dumbbell Shrugs | 3 | 15 | Straight up, hold 1 sec at top, no rolling |
| 7 | TRX Row or Assisted Pull-Up Machine | 3 | 10 | Full stretch at bottom, full contraction at top |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Lat stretch (doorway or hanging) — 30 sec each side
- Child's pose — 45 sec
- Thoracic rotation stretch — 30 sec each side
- Upper trap stretch (tilt head to shoulder) — 30 sec each side

---

## Friday — Arms & Biceps (Biceps · Forearms · Light Triceps)

> A lighter day. Enough to build arm strength without exhausting you before the weekend's evening session.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- 5 min light elliptical
- Wrist circles — 10 each direction
- Arm swings across body × 15

**Main Workout (42 min)**

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dumbbell Bicep Curl | 3 | 15 | Full range, don't swing elbows forward |
| 2 | Hammer Curl | 3 | 15 | Neutral grip, targets brachialis |
| 3 | Cable Bicep Curl | 3 | 15 | Constant tension, slow negatives |
| 4 | Incline Dumbbell Curl | 3 | 12 | Full stretch at bottom |
| 5 | EZ-Bar or Barbell Curl | 3 | 12 | Elbows stay tucked at sides |
| 6 | Wrist Curls (dumbbell, seated) | 3 | 20 | Forearm rests on thigh, full range |
| 7 | Tricep Overhead Rope Extension (cable) | 3 | 15 | Light weight — keeps arms balanced |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Bicep wall stretch — 30 sec each arm
- Wrist flexor stretch (arm extended, press fingers back) — 30 sec each
- Wrist extensor stretch (arm extended, press fingers down) — 30 sec each
- Forearm self-massage (use opposite thumb) — 1 min each arm

---

## Saturday — Full Body Light Circuit (Active Recovery Day)

> Lower intensity. Keeps the body moving without overloading. Great for building work capacity and burning calories lightly.

**Warm-up (8 min)**
- 5 min treadmill walk/jog
- Full body dynamic stretch: neck → shoulders → hips → legs

**Main Workout — 3 Rounds Circuit (42 min)**
45 seconds work · 15 seconds rest · 60 seconds rest between rounds

| # | Exercise | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bodyweight Squat | 45 sec | Slow and controlled |
| 2 | Push-Up (or knee push-up) | 45 sec | Quality over quantity |
| 3 | TRX Row | 45 sec | Body at 45°, row to chest |
| 4 | Reverse Lunge (bodyweight) | 45 sec | Alternate legs |
| 5 | Plank Hold | 45 sec | Breathe steadily |
| 6 | Dumbbell Shoulder Press (light) | 45 sec | 3–5 kg dumbbells |
| 7 | Step Touches or Jumping Jacks | 45 sec | Keep heart rate gentle |

**Cool-down (10 min)**
- Full body static stretch routine — hold each stretch 30 sec
- Focus on any tight areas from the week

---

## Sunday — REST DAY

**No gym. This is not optional.**

The body builds muscle during recovery, not during the workout. Use Sunday to:
- Sleep in (maintain at least 8 hours)
- Eat a nutritious meal with protein, carbs, and healthy fats
- Hydrate well
- Take a light walk if restless
- Meal prep or grocery trip (Publix run — check stock)
- Spend time on hobbies, call home, relax

## Evening Cardio — Daily (17:30–18:30)

This is a **separate** session from the morning gym. Rotate through the following each day to avoid monotony:

| Activity | Suggested Days | Where at UCF |
|---|---|---|
| Jogging / Running | Mon · Wed · Fri | Campus paths, RWC Park |
| Cycling | Tue · Thu | Campus cycle routes |
| Swimming | Sat | RWC Lap Pool (Mon–Fri: 06:00–19:00 · Sat: 11:00–17:00) |
| Walk + Explore Campus | Any | Anywhere — cafes, new spots |

> On days you feel heavy from morning training, reduce evening intensity — a brisk walk counts. Consistency beats perfection.

## RWC Instructor Sessions for Beginners

### Step 1 — Fitness Assessment (Do This First)
Before starting this plan independently, book a **free baseline Fitness Assessment** with an RWC Certified Personal Trainer. They will measure your body composition, assess your current fitness level, and give you personalised recommendations.

- **Book here:** https://ucf.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_2uFJIOmriZfGWhw
- **Location:** RWC Main — Floor 1, adjacent to Basketball Court #1

---

### Step 2 — Personal Training (Highly Recommended for First 4–6 Weeks)
All RWC Personal Trainers hold national certifications (ACE, ACSM, ISSA, NASM, NSCA). They will build a custom program for your exact goals, teach proper form, and keep you accountable.

- **New clients:** Complete the Initial Consultation first (includes fitness assessment + goal-setting)
- **Availability:** RWC Main — by appointment | RWC Downtown — walk-ins welcome
- **Contact:** fitness@ucf.edu · 407-823-2408

---

### Step 3 — Group Exercise Classes (Free — Great for Motivation)
The RWC runs **60+ instructor-led classes per week**, all free for enrolled UCF students. Reserve a spot 24 hours before class at https://ucfrwc.org (sign in with UCF NID). Arrive 15 minutes early.

**Recommended classes for this student:**

| Class | Why It's Good for You |
|---|---|
| **KnightFit** | Full-body functional training — the best beginner class at the RWC |
| **Cycle / Cycle + Strength** | Low-impact cardio + leg strength — perfect complement to morning gym |
| **Pilates** | Core strength, posture, and flexibility — pairs perfectly with Monday core day |
| **Yoga** | Recovery, breathing, and mental focus — ideal as a Saturday add-on |
| **HIIT** | Stamina builder — start this after 4–6 weeks once base fitness is established |
| **Barre** | Core and lower body stability — good cross-training |

## Key Reminders

- Eat a light snack with protein 30–45 min before the morning session (e.g., banana + peanut butter, or a boiled egg)
- Bring a full water bottle — drink between every set
- Warm-up and cool-down are not optional — they protect you from injury
- Never train to failure as a beginner — stop 2–3 reps before you cannot continue
- Log your weights in a small notebook or your phone — progressive overload is the key to getting stronger
- If something hurts (not just burn/fatigue), stop and visit the RWC Athletic Training Room (Tue–Thu: 13:30–17:00)
