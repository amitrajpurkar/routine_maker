# Amit's Weekly Workout Plan
### 12-Week Weight Loss & Strength Program — Home Equipment Edition

> **Profile:** Male, 52 years old · 5'7" (170 cm) · 185 lb (84 kg) · Waist 35 in
> **Goal (12 weeks):** 165 lb · Waist ≤ 32 in · Increased strength & stamina
> **Current baseline:** 4:30 AM jog, 3–4 miles, 4x/week (~60 mi/month) — this is the only current workout
> **Equipment:** Home only — dumbbells, resistance bands, (bench/chair optional). No gym/machines.
> **Additional time available:** 20–30 min, 3x/week, added directly onto existing jog days
> **Real problem areas (per Amit):** eating habits, insufficient water intake, inconsistent sleep (can't get to bed by 9 PM)

> **Reference:** Track mileage, pace, and heart-rate trends on your Nike Running Dashboard: https://amitrajpurkar.github.io/static/nike-dashboard.html — use it monthly to confirm your ~60 mi/month volume holds steady while pace should gradually improve as weight drops.

## 1. Why This Plan Is Built This Way

Your jogging base is already solid and consistent — 4x/week at 3–4 miles is a real habit, not something to mess with. The 20 lb / 3 in gap between where you are and where you want to be in 12 weeks won't close from more running alone; it closes from three things layered on top of what you already do:

1. **Strength training** (new) — preserves and builds muscle while you're in a calorie deficit, raises resting metabolism, and is the #1 lever for "increased strength" specifically.
2. **Nutrition discipline** — the single biggest lever for the scale and the waistband. No amount of jogging outruns a bad diet.
3. **Hydration + sleep** — both directly affect appetite regulation, recovery, and whether the deficit above actually sticks. You named these as your real problem areas, so they get equal billing here, not an afterthought.

**A note on pace:** losing 20 lb in 12 weeks averages ~1.6–1.7 lb/week, which is at the upper edge of what's normally considered sustainable (1–2 lb/week). It's achievable with consistent execution on all four fronts below, but if you have any cardiac, joint, or other health considerations, get a quick check-up/clearance before ramping up training load — standard prudent advice at 52 starting a new strength regimen. None of the guidance below is personalized medical or dietetic advice.

## 3. Strength Circuits (Home Equipment: Dumbbells + Bands)

**Format for all three circuits:** 3 sets per exercise · 12–15 reps (or time noted) · 60–75 sec rest between sets · stop 2–3 reps before failure. Increase dumbbell weight by 2.5–5 lb only once you complete all sets with good form for 2 sessions in a row.

### Tuesday & Thursday — Mobility & Recovery (15 min)

Not a rest-from-movement day — a rest-from-loading day. This protects your joints and keeps you limber for the jog days.

- Dynamic hip circles + leg swings — 10 each direction/side
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5/side
- Hip flexor kneeling stretch — 30 sec/side
- Foam roll or hand-massage calves, hamstrings, IT band — 3–4 min
- Optional: 20–30 min easy walk on Thursday if you want extra movement without load

---

### Saturday — Long Jog (No Added Strength)

Keep this day as pure cardio recovery from the week's strength load. Full stretch cool-down after: quads, hamstrings, calves, hip flexors, lower back — 30 sec each.

### Sunday — Full Rest

No training. Sleep in if you can, hydrate, meal-prep for the week, light walk only if restless.

## 4. Hydration Plan (Target: ~3.0–3.3 L / 100–110 oz per day)

Your stated problem area — fix it with a fixed schedule rather than "drinking when thirsty," since thirst is an unreliable signal by the time you notice it.

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| On waking (before 4:30 AM jog) | 300–400 ml water immediately |
| Right after jog | 200–300 ml water (more on hot/humid days) |
| Breakfast | 1 full glass with the meal |
| Mid-morning | Refill and finish a 500 ml bottle |
| Lunch | 1 full glass with the meal |
| Afternoon | Refill and finish a second 500 ml bottle |
| Dinner | 1 full glass with the meal |
| After ~7:30 PM | Taper fluids — sip only, to avoid disrupting sleep |

**Rule of thumb:** if you're not drinking anything between 4:30 AM and lunch besides coffee, you're already behind for the day. Keep a marked bottle at your desk as a visual tracker.

## 5. Sleep Plan (Target: Lights-out by 9:00–9:15 PM)

Waking at 4:30 AM means an 8-hour night requires being asleep by 8:30 PM — genuinely difficult. A **consistent 9:00–9:15 PM lights-out** gets you ~7–7.25 hours, which is a large, realistic upgrade over an irregular schedule, even if it's short of the textbook 8.

| Time | Wind-down step |
|---|---|
| 8:15 PM | Stop screens/work email — dim the lights |
| 8:15–8:35 PM | Light stretch or short walk · lay out tomorrow's jog clothes & fill water bottle |
| 8:35–8:50 PM | Shower/hygiene · brief non-stimulating reading |
| 8:50–9:00 PM | Lights out |

- **Caffeine cutoff:** none after 2:00 PM
- **Consistency:** same lights-out time on weekends too (±30 min max) — this is what actually resets a sleep pattern, more than any single night's duration
- Treat this as a habit to build in Phase 1 (see below), not something that fixes itself once the workouts start

## 6. Eating Guardrails (Not a Full Meal Plan — Just the Rules That Matter)

- **Rough daily calorie target: ~1,900–2,050 kcal.** Estimated maintenance (TDEE) for your stats and activity level is roughly 2,550–2,650 kcal/day, so this range creates the deficit needed for the 12-week goal. This is a general estimate, not personalized — adjust it up or down based on your actual weekly weigh-in trend (see tracker below).
- **Protein target: ~130–150 g/day** (roughly 0.8 g per lb of your goal bodyweight), spread across meals (~30–40 g each) — protects muscle while you're in a deficit and keeps you fuller.
- **Half the plate = vegetables/fruit** at lunch and dinner.
- **Cut liquid calories** — no sugary soda/juice; water, black coffee, or unsweetened tea only.
- **Carb timing** — keep starchy carbs concentrated around the morning jog (small pre-run snack + post-run meal), lighter on carbs at dinner.
- **Dinner cutoff by 7:30–8:00 PM** — aligns with the sleep wind-down and avoids going to bed on a full stomach.
- **Prep ahead** — batch-cook or plan meals once a week (e.g., Sunday) so decisions aren't made hungry and impulsive; this is the single biggest fix for "eating habits" as a problem area.
- **One flexible meal per week** is fine — it supports long-term adherence rather than undermining it.

## 9. Key Reminders

- Get a quick medical check-up/clearance before ramping up training load, given your age and the pace of this goal — sensible, not optional.
- Warm-up and cool-down are not optional at 52 — joint health matters more now than it did at 25.
- Progressive overload only when form is solid for 2 consecutive sessions — never chase weight over form.
- Track mileage and pace on your Nike dashboard monthly — confirm volume holds near 60 mi/month and watch pace trend as weight drops.
- A missed strength session is not a failure — get back on schedule at the next one. Consistency over the 12 weeks beats any single perfect week.
- Sleep and hydration aren't "extra" — they're load-bearing parts of this plan, not afterthoughts.
